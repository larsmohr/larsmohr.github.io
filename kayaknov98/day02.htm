Day 2, Monday, November 2, 1998
University of Washington to Edmonds.
17.2 miles.

Notes
At the locks I waited at the holding pier for the small locks. I worried about how I'd know what to do. Eventually, the upstream doors opened and a parade of yachts motored past me. They were going slow enough, so most asked where was I was going and offered encouragement. One woman, on hearing I'd try for Vancouver Island, shouted "eat a lot!".

When the last boat passed, I heard something over the loudspeaker, but couldn't make it out. I paddled forward into the channel to get closer. Then I heard "kayaker, clear the channel, I'd like to get the fishing boat in first". I couldn't hear a boat, and it is pretty difficult to look behind me, so I was surprised that there was a boat. I backed up and it passed me on the left. The fishermen also asked me what I was up to.

I paused then moved slowly forward. When I passed into the lock's chamber, the attendant said "You need to be aware of what's happening behind you.". To my surprise, just then another fishing boat came by on my left. I'll need to get a rearview mirror like a sculler or biker users.

Still no designated camp site tonight. Had to do what I could. I won't be at a campground until Day 5.

Log Entry
I skipped the oatmeal breakfast and made an effort to get started quickly. I started paddling around 7:30am. Went through the Montlake Cut and stopped for water at Gasworks Park. Next was the fishing/industrial area that ended with the Hiram M. Chittenden Locks. My Marine VHF radio's Ewa WaterSound bag lost its clip when I pulled it out and water got #@&% into the radio! After the locks, I paddled to Golden Garden's park and took the radio apart to dry it. Lost a @#$%& screw while I was at it. Good thing was, when I put is back together again, it partly worked. Worst case, a $130 lesson. I even doubted that damn clip back home. Should have acted on that feeling and taped it in position.

On leaving Golden Garden Park at 1:15p, I did a long stretch across a bay to Pt. Wells and then finally beached at Edmonds 3hrs later. Felt pretty good after having adjusted the seat! I stuffed a pair of pants behind the backrest. I called Sylvia and left a message. Then I was off to find a spot to spend the night. It will be nice when I can have a regular camp site! Soon. We're getting to the good stuff.

This is really beautiful. Calm water, stars are out, ferries are milling about on the water. Full moon tonight. Time to pour over the maps and do my other chores.
