21.4 miles.

Notes
Sylvia helped me roll the boat and carry the gear down the trail behind the house to the Sammamish River (picture #2). At the river, the bank was steep enough that we used the tow line to belay the kayak down to the water. At 9:15am I was underway with a heavy load - low in the water, but very stable. Just minutes after launch is a shallow rapids, and I hit a rock that, days later, would be discovered to have caused through-hull damage and a leak! The forward hold was leaking at a rate of about a cup every 6 hours.

Being Sunday, I met a group of three kayakers near St. Edwards State Park. At the UW docks, I called Sylvia. She wanted to come pick me up already! I had to make-do for a spot to sleep.

Log Entry
Long day paddling. It was a luxury to listen to KPLU using the walkman in the evening. I'm feeling real sore now, after eating dinner. Neck, shoulders. My right hand's finger's tingle a little.
